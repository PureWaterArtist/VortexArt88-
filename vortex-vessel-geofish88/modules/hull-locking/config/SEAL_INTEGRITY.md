# Module hull-locking: Quality Control Gasket Compression & Leak Integrity Protocols

This document details the field-level check processes and laboratory test boundaries required to certify the watertightness of the hardshell folding seams.

---

## 🔍 Pre-Launch Field Compression Audit

Before launching the watercraft into active fishing waters, the operator must execute a manual visual sweep across all perimeter joints to verify uniform gasket compression:

1.  **Inspect Latch Draw Alignment:** Open the hull to 100% expansion and latch all **316 Stainless Steel Over-Center Draw Latches**. Verify that the hook loop catches the strike plate cleanly without side-binding.
2.  **Verify Bulb Compression Margin:** Use manual sliding calipers to measure the seam gap thickness right next to each latch point. The nominal compressed distance must match the target clearance bounds:
    $$\text{Target Thickness} = 2.60\text{ mm} \pm 0.15\text{ mm}$$
3.  **Audit EPDM Continuity:** Inspect the continuous co-extruded hollow bulb tracks. Ensure there are zero gravel grains, dirt particles, or tears across the sealing faces to preserve the 100% hermetic barrier.

---

## 🔬 Laboratory Vacuum Decay Leak Protocol

For advanced cleanroom batch certification, the fully assembled hull must pass a standard hydrostatic vacuum decay audit to guarantee no localized structural weeping can occur:

*   **Vacuum Pressure Application:** Attach local suction cups across a mock joint section and apply a steady drawing pressure of $-20.0\text{ kPa}$.
*   **Decay Threshold:** Seal the vacuum valve line. The maximum allowable pressure drop decay over a continuous 5-minute testing window must not exceed $0.05\text{ kPa}$. Any decay rate past this threshold indicates micro-fissure leaking along the live-hinge boundaries.
