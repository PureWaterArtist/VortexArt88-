# Module hull-seating: Bulkhead Locking & Accessory Track Alignment Protocols

This document details the step-by-step validation checks and maximum safety loads required to securely mount internal seating and gear rails without warping the folding hull.

---

## 🪑 Pre-Launch Bulkhead Seat Insertion Checklist

Before launching, the operator must verify that the load-bearing seat is fully seated to lock the origami folds into a rigid state:

1.  **Clear the Floor Tracks:** Ensure the vertical interlocking polymer floor tracks are free of sand, weeds, or debris that could block the seat tabs.
2.  **Slide Seat to Detent Lock:** Insert the pre-molded $HDPE$ bulkhead seat squarely into the left and right side channels. Push downward firmly until the integrated spring tabs click into the bottom detents.
3.  **Verify Transverse Counter-Bracing:** Check the outer hull panels adjacent to the seat. The skin must be pushed outward to its maximum design width with zero bulging, ensuring the seat is actively countering external water compression.

---

## 🎣 Accessory Track Load & T-Bolt Mounting Limits

To prevent localized cracking or stress fracturing of the upper $4.0\text{ mm}$ copolymer polypropylene panel lips during heavy rod pulling or trolling maneuvers, all track mounts must follow these load constraints:

*   **Maximum Lever Torque:** Gear track mounts must not exceed a maximum dynamic torsional bending moment of $45.0\text{ N-m}$. Trolling rod holders must be backed with load-distribution washers.
*   **T-Bolt Seating Force:** Tighten all accessory T-bolts to an hand-tight maximum torque limit of:
    $$\text{Target Tightening Torque} = 2.5\text{ N-m} \pm 0.2\text{ N-m}$$
    Exceeding this value will strip the co-extruded aluminum track track channels out of the polymer hull flange.
