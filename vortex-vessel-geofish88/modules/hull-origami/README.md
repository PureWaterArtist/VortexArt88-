# Project GEO-FISH-v88: Origami Hull Layout & Crease Architecture (Module: hull-origami)

## 📐 System Manifest & Metamaterial Folding Philosophy

The **Origami Hull Layout & Crease Architecture Module (Project GEO-FISH-Origami)** houses the geometric data sheets, manufacturing scoring depths, and spatial transformation paths for the continuous skin of the watercraft. Instead of welding multiple individual panels together, the entire hull shell is produced from a single, unbroken topological surface of $4.0\text{ mm}$ high-density copolymer polypropylene.

By engraving a highly precise, non-Euclidean **Miura-ori tessellation tessellation grid** directly onto the sheet, the flat material transforms into a predictable kinetic structure. This layout ensures that applying tension to the front and rear corner grab-handles forces every mountain fold and valley fold to swing open simultaneously. This action effortlessly transitions the boat from its stowed car trunk configuration into an $8.5\text{-foot}$ rigid fishing hull with zero independent loose parts.
