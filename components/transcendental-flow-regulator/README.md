# π/Φ Transcendental Flow Regulator

The **π/Φ Transcendental Flow Regulator** is a hybrid physical-digital component designed to bridge abstract geometric invariants with physical fluid dynamics. By deploying a dual-channel framework, this module splits incoming high-velocity mass (fluid or data) into two nested, counter-rotating streams that naturally eliminate turbulence, skin friction, and packet collisions through systemic mechanical balance.

## 📐 Core Architecture & Geometry

This component relies on two eternal mathematical constants to govern its internal spatial geometry:

* **The Outer Channel (Φ):** The boundary wall follows a clockwise logarithmic spiral scaled by the Golden Ratio (≈ 1.618). This handles high-volume perimeter flow, allowing it to glide along the boundary layer with minimal skin friction.
* **The Inner Core (2π):** The central core houses an inverted, counter-clockwise helical cylinder shaped by circular geometry (2π) to induce an equal and opposite fluid torque.
* **The Intersection:** At the exit nozzle, the opposing rotational streams converge. Because the geometric ratios are transcendental, the vectors lock together like interlocking gears, canceling out macro-turbulence without physical friction.

## 🛠 Manufacturing Profile

To maintain the frictionless boundary layer required by the logarithmic spiral, builders must adhere to the strict production standards outlined in `regulator-config.json`:
* **Wall Loops Minimum:** 5 (To withstand dynamic internal pressure loops).
* **Infill Pattern:** Gyroid (For optimal anisotropic internal load stabilization).
* **Post-Processing (Mandatory):** FDM (filament) 3D prints introduce micro-stepping ridges. It is highly recommended to apply vapor smoothing (ABS/ASA) or a thin internal food-safe epoxy coating (PETG) to suppress microscopic surface-layer skin friction.
