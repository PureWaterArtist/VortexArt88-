# Project REPULSINE: 4-Day Cleanroom Prototyping & Assembly Protocol
**Configuration:** Version 2.0.0 // 2-Passenger Compact Implosion Platform

This document provides the clear, optimized, step-by-step industrial workflows required to manufacture and assemble the compact airframe fuselage, counter-rotating wavy corrugated spin-discs, and fluid computer blocks from raw, non-proprietary elements.

---

## 🖨️ Day 1: Multi-Axial SLA Fuselage & Disc Printing
1.  **Prepare Resin Substrate Vats:** Fill the cleanroom industrial micro-SLA vats with a non-toxic composite liquid consisting of 45% ultra-fine Fused-Quartz Silicon powder and 55% Crystalline Nanocellulose (CNC) liquid tough resin suspension matrix.
2.  **Initiate Layer Slicing Run:** Load the compact 2-passenger 3D CAD models. Calibrate the UV laser to a 25-micron spot size. Print the main 3.2-meter monocoque fuselage, the concentric wavy ridged spin-discs following the 1:1.618 golden spiral layout, and the 2.2mm computer channels simultaneously. (Time: 12 Hours continuous run).
3.  **Evacuation Flush & Ultrasonic Bath:** Submerge the printed parts in a high-purity isopropyl alcohol wash. Engage ultrasonic agitations to completely clear out uncured resin from the internal micro-grooves and intake plenums. (Time: 3 Hours).
4.  **Photolytic Cross-Linking Pass:** Transfer components to a UV-thermal curing oven stabilized at 60°C to lock all structural dimensions and remove interior micro-stresses. (Time: 6 Hours).

---

## 🛰️ Day 2: CVD Graphene Deposition, Sputtering, & Solar Skin Application
1.  **Graphene Boundary Layer Coating:** Utilize Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) to deposit a single atomic layer of high-purity Graphene cleanly across the wavy grooves of the spin-discs and internal 2.2mm logic tracks to enforce a 98% drag-free super-slip floor. (Time: 8 Hours).
2.  **Node Metallization Pass:** Mask the non-conductive tracks and mount the substrate blocks inside a Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) vacuum tank. Sputter high-purity gold to grow the low-voltage control tracks and internal actuation node pads to a depth of $1500\text{ \AA}$. (Time: 4 Hours).
3.  **Dielectric Spin-Coating:** Spin-coat an amorphous fluoropolymer Cytop resin layer evenly over the gold control paths. Vacuum-bake at 80°C to secure a seamless 1.2-micron insulating shield with a 15V dielectric breakdown barrier. (Time: 4 Hours).
4.  **Solar Film Deposition:** Spray-apply the organic, non-toxic Perovskite-Chlorophyll photovoltaic matrix directly onto the vehicle’s aerodynamic outer biopolymer fuselage skin. Heat-seal to verify a continuous $220\text{ W/m}^2$ radiant solar harvesting yield. (Time: 8 Hours).

---

## 🛠️ Day 3: Component Integration, Transducer Bonding, & Coaxial Hub Interlock
1.  **Piezoelectric Ribbon Inlay:** Position the 250-micron quartz-crystal piezoelectric ribbons inside the dead-end deceleration tracts. Secure the flexible PVDF ribbon carpets flush across the 2-passenger seating bases and the 150-liter compact cargo cell. (Time: 6 Hours).
2.  **Electro-Acoustic Array Mounting:** Install the standalone 2,400 Hz acoustic transducers flanking the internal water lines. Interlink the nodes directly with the floor-mounted graphene ultracapacitor loop to manage the permanent standing harmonic anti-freeze ripple. (Time: 6 Hours).
3.  **Casing Interlock and Thruster Integration:** Mount the counter-rotating spin-discs onto the central coaxial drive hub using zero-tool mechanical lock-tabs. Pot all external wire ports inside a protective bio-rubber compound seal. (Time: 8 Hours).

---

## 🧪 Day 4: Vacuum Leak Decay Audit & Isothermal Implosion Flight Sync
1.  **Hydrostatic Joint Decay Verification:** Seal the primary logic track manifolds. Draw a deep vacuum of $-101.3\text{ kPa}$ and lock the line. Pressure decay loss must register completely flat ($\leq 0.05\text{ kPa}$) over a continuous 12-hour audit hold to certify a zero-consumption closed loop. (Time: 12 Hours).
2.  **Acoustic & Initial Motion Charging:** Vacuum-inject exactly 80.0 Liters of pure distilled water into the internal logic circuits and anti-freeze manifolds. Deliver a starting pulse to spin the central coaxial hub to the critical 12,500 RPM threshold. (Time: 4 Hours).
3.  **Implosion Validation Check:** Activate the 2,400 Hz acoustic array to verify winter shield performance (42.50 Watts). Verify that under high-speed centripetal spin, the incoming atmospheric air mass undergoes continuous density collapse at exactly $4^\circ\text{C}$ along the graphene grooves, tearing open a severe, localized partial vacuum of $-101.3\text{ kPa}$ to achieve vertical lift-off with zero cavitation. System cleared for flight. (Time: 8 Hours).
