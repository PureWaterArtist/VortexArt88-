## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Clinical Circuit Assembly

### Step 1: MHD Propulsion Voltage Clamping
1. Route the positive power rail coming from your subcutaneous wireless charging battery pad to the **IN** pin of the **TLV75533P LDO Regulator (REG1)**. 
2. Solder the negative return rail from the battery directly to the **GND** pin of REG1 to establish your common system ground.
3. Route the regulated **OUT** pin of REG1 straight to the positive terminal of the **ARVH-02 Glassy-Carbon Electrode Track**. This locks your propulsion voltage at a clean, non-fluctuating **3.3V DC** maximum ceiling, ensuring that no sudden battery spikes can transfer heat to the passing blood column.

### Step 2: Muscle Compression Capture & Rectification
1. Route the two thin, insulated output traces coming from the outer **PVDF Piezoelectric Muscular Casing** into the circuit enclosure box.
2. Solder these two AC lines directly to the alternating input pins (~ ~) of the sub-miniature **HD01-T Full-Wave Bridge Rectifier (BR1)**. This converts the physical thumping of your surrounding chest-wall muscles into direct current.

### Step 3: Energy Filtering & Surge Clamping
1. Connect the positive (+) output pin of BR1 to the positive post of the **22uF Solid Tantalum Capacitor (C1)**. Connect its negative pin to the common ground rail. 
2. Place the **PESD3V3 Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS1)** directly in parallel across C1. This safely absorbs and short-circuits any sudden micro-static friction charges generated by the spinning fluid core before they can drift into the delicate pacing components.

### Step 4: Battery Management Connection
1. Route the smoothed positive output line from C1 straight into the trickle-charge input pin of your internal **Battery Management IC**.
2. Connect the ground return line to the common system ground post. As the patient breathes and contracts their chest muscles, the high-voltage piezo pulses are smoothly down-regulated, forming a closed energy loop that extends overall runtime between wireless recharging cycles.
