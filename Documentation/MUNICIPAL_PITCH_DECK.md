# Project Aetheris: Municipal Pitch Deck
### *Transforming Civil Stormwater Infrastructure into Public Art & Natural Utility*

---

## 🛑 Slide 1: The Problem — The Cost of Climate Extremes
*   **The Flash Flood Crisis:** Traditional stormwater infrastructure is binary. It channels water out of sight through concrete pipes or overflows into streets, causing catastrophic property damage.
*   **The Chemical Burden:** Municipal water management heavily relies on ongoing expenditures for chlorine, chemical coagulants, and constant energy-intensive backwashing of massive sand filters.
*   **The Invisible Asset:** Water treatment facilities are typically hidden away behind razor wire, contributing zero aesthetic or recreational value to the communities funding them.

---

## 🌀 Slide 2: The Solution — Vortex Meadow Park
*   **Passive Landscape Funneling:** Utilizing contoured permaculture swales, the park’s grading naturally slows down, de-energizes, and absorbs high-volume flash flood waters.
*   **Filter-Free Mechanical Sorting:** The water is channeled into a centralized, sub-surface concrete figure-8 chamber where fluid mechanics do the work of a traditional multi-million dollar treatment plant.
*   **Civic Landmark:** By turning a utility facility into an open-air public amphitheater featuring an illuminated, glowing water singularity, infrastructure becomes the focal point of community pride and eco-tourism.

---

## 🧬 Slide 3: How the Physics Protects the Budget
*   **Zero-Disposables De-Grit:** High-velocity tangential inflow creates extreme centrifugal forces. Sand, silt, and heavy urban debris are pinned to the outer walls and ejected through a passive perimeter trench. *Eliminates disposable filter costs.*
*   **Biomimetic Aeration:** Counter-rotating vortex currents collide at a central geometric null point, generating a massive pressure drop. This vacuum sucks atmospheric air deep into the water column, spiking Dissolved Oxygen (DO) levels. *Naturally breaks down organic waste without chemicals.*
*   **Kinetic Microbe Shearing:** Forcing water through precise geometric contractions at high velocities subjects pathogens to mechanical shear stress, destroying anaerobic cell walls without toxic chlorine.
