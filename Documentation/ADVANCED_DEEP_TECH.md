## 🚀 1. Zero-Gravity Fluid Management (Spacecraft Fuel & Life Support)
*   **The Deep-Tech Problem:** In the weightlessness of orbit, liquids don't stay at the bottom of a tank—they float around in random, unpredictable blobs. Because of this, spacecraft life-support loops or liquid rocket engines can suck in pockets of empty gas (vapor locks), which can stall life-support pumps or cause catastrophic engine explosions during mid-orbit restarts.
*   **The Vortex Solution:** You don't need planetary gravity to guide fluid; you can use centripetal force. By driving life-support water or liquid rocket propellant through our golden-ratio mirror nozzles inside a spacecraft tank, we create a stable, artificial internal gravity gradient. The centripetal force slings the heavy liquid outward against the tank walls, while the central singularity axis perfectly separates out the gas vapors. This ensures a continuous, **bubble-free liquid feed to spacecraft pumps in zero-G** without relying on moving mechanical pistons that can jam in deep space.
