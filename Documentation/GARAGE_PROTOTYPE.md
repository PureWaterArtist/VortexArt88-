# 🛠️ The Low-Tech "Two-Bucket" Garage Prototype

This blueprint details how to build a fully functional, transparent Twin-Vortex Singularity testing engine using basic tools and low-cost materials available at any local hardware store. This guide is optimized for makers, students, and off-grid builders working with a minimal budget.

---

## 🛒 Hardware Store Shopping List

### 📦 The Core Chambers
*   **2x Five-Gallon Clear Plastic Buckets:** Standard transparent utility pails. (Must be completely clear to visually track the dual vortex geometry).
*   **1x Marine-Grade 100% Silicone Sealant:** For joining the cut bucket windows into a water-tight seam.
*   **1x Pack of 1/4-inch Stainless Steel Bolts, Nuts, and Washers:** To mechanically clamp the bucket windows together while curing.

### 🚰 The Symmetrical Plumbing Tree
*   **3x 1-1/2 inch PVC Slip Ball Valves:** One master valve to regulate pump flow, and two branch valves to micro-balance the water pressure.
*   **1x 1-1/2 inch PVC T-Fitting:** Splits the pump main into your twin input branches.
*   **4x 1-1/2 inch 90-Degree PVC Elbows:** Guides the water at a sharp tangential angle into the top outer edges of the buckets.
*   **1x 10-Foot Length of 1-1/2 inch Schedule 40 PVC Pipe:** Standard plumbing conduit.
*   **1x Clear PVC Primer and Solvent Cement Glue:** To fuse the pipe manifold tree.
*   **2x 1-1/2 inch PVC Bulkhead Fittings:** Screwed into the bottom center of each bucket to serve as your gravity drains.

### 🌀 The Catchment & Propulsion
*   **1x 27-Gallon Heavy-Duty Plastic Storage Tote (Black with Yellow Lid):** Serves as your water reservoir sitting underneath your workbench.
*   **1x 1000 GPH to 1200 GPH Submersible Pond Pump:** Placed inside the lower reservoir tote to continuously cycle the water back up to your printed nozzles.
*   **1x 1-1/2 inch Female NPT to PVC Slip Adapter:** Conforming the pump output to your pipe network.

## 📐 Fabrication Blueprint: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Precision Window Cutting
To allow the two spinning water walls to collide and form the singularity without ruining the structural roundness of the buckets, you must cut a central communication window:
*   **Window Dimensions:** 4 inches wide by 10 inches tall.
*   **Placement:** Draw this rectangle perfectly centered on the flattest side of each bucket, starting exactly 3.5 inches up from the inside bottom floor.
*   **Cutting:** Drill a 1/2-inch pilot hole inside each of the four inner corners of your marked rectangle. Insert a fine-toothed manual jigsaw blade and cut carefully along your lines. Smooth any rough plastic edges with fine sandpaper.

```text
             🔒 CLAMP & BOLT PATTERN (1-inch Spacing)
       
                   o   o   o   o   o
                 ┌───────────────────┐
                 │                   │
               o │   4" WIDE WINDOW  │ o
               o │         │         │ o
               o │  10"    X <Null   │ o
               o │  TALL   │  Point  │ o
               o │                   │ o
                 └───────────────────┘
                   o   o   o   o   o
```

### Step 2: The Perimeter Bolt Ring
*   Clamp the two buckets tightly back-to-back exactly how they will be configured.
*   Mark a dot for a bolt hole **1 inch away from the window edge** every 2 inches all the way around the rectangle.
*   Using a **9/32-inch Step Drill Bit (Uni-bit)** or a Brad-Point plastic drill bit, drill straight through *both* buckets simultaneously at your marked dots. 
*   *Warning:* Never use standard twist metal drill bits on hard clear plastic pails, as they will grab and violently shatter the material.

### Step 3: Marine Sealing & Clamping
*   Separate the buckets and clean the cut faces.
*   Apply a thick, continuous 1/2-inch bead of **marine-grade 100% silicone** directly between the bolt-hole ring and the cut window edge on the outside of both buckets.
*   Press the buckets back-to-back. Slide your 1/4-inch stainless steel bolts through the holes (utilizing washers on both sides to distribute pressure). 
*   Hand-tighten the nuts until you see the silicone squeeze out evenly all around the seam. **Do not over-tighten with a wrench or the plastic will stress-crack.**
*   Let the silicone cure completely for 24 to 48 hours before testing.

### Step 4: Fabricating the Balanced Plumbing Manifold
Cut your 10-foot PVC pipe into the following balanced segments to guarantee identical fluid friction on both sides:
*   **Pump Main:** 24 inches — Vertical lift from the reservoir pump to the master ball valve.
*   **Upper T-Feeder:** 12 inches — Connects the master valve to the bottom of your T-Junction.
*   **Symmetrical Wings:** 2x 18 inches — Extend horizontally out of the left and right sides of the T-Junction.
*   **Nozzle Drops:** 2x 8 inches — Drop vertically out of your branch ball valves to connect to the 90-degree elbows.

```text
                        [ From Pump ]
                              │
                        [ Master Valve ] (Total Flow Control)
                              │
                          [ PVC T ]
                         /         \
          [ Symmetrical Wing ]     [ Symmetrical Wing ]
                 (18")                    (18")
                   │                        │
            [ Branch Valve A ]       [ Branch Valve B ]
                   │                        │
             [ Nozzle Drop ]          [ Nozzle Drop ]
                   │                        │
             [ Mirror Nozzle A]       [ Mirror Nozzle B]
                (Clockwise)          (Counter-Clockwise)
```

## 🎛️ Live Calibration Protocol
1. Fill your lower 27-gallon reservoir tote with clean water, open all three PVC ball valves entirely, and switch on the submersible pump.
2. Slowly adjust the master valve until both buckets fill and start spinning into deep, stable vortex funnels.
3. Observe the center window cutout zone. If the water is chaotic or sloshing unevenly, notice which vortex is physically wider. 
4. Slightly choke the individual branch valve on that dominant side to balance the opposing pressures. 
5. Continue fine-tuning until the central collision zone calms completely and locks into a flat, glass-like, unmoving plane of water—your functioning workbench singularity.
