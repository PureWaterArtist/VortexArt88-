# Project AETHERIS: 4-Day Cleanroom Manufacturing & Streamlined Assembly Protocol

This document provides the clear, optimized, step-by-step industrial workflows required to manufacture and assemble the vehicle chassis and propulsion core from raw, non-proprietary elements.

---

## 🖨️ Day 1: Multi-Axial SLA Monocoque Printing & Post-Curing
1.  **Prepare Resin Substrate Vats:** Fill the cleanroom industrial micro-SLA vats with a non-toxic composite liquid consisting of 45% ultra-fine Fused-Quartz Silicon powder and 55% Crystalline Nanocellulose (CNC) liquid tough resin suspension matrix.
2.  **Initiate Layer Slicing Run:** Load the 3D CAD data. Calibrate the UV laser to a 25-micron spot size. Print the main 5-passenger monocoque frame, internal logarithmic spiral lattice nodes, and 2.2mm logic tracks simultaneously. (Time: 14 Hours continuous run).
3.  **Evacuation Flush & Ultrasonic Bath:** Submerge the printed hull parts in a high-purity isopropyl alcohol wash. Engage ultrasonic agitations to completely clear out uncured resin from the internal Venturi intakes and fluidic channels. (Time: 4 Hours).
4.  **Photolytic Cross-Linking Pass:** Transfer components to a UV-thermal curing oven stabilized at 60°C to lock all structural dimensions and remove interior micro-stresses. (Time: 6 Hours).

---

## 🛰️ Day 2: PVD Sputtering, Insulation Coating, & Solar Skin Application
1.  **Node Metallization Pass:** Mask the non-conductive channels and mount the substrate blocks inside a Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) vacuum tank. Sputter high-purity gold to grow the low-voltage control tracks and internal actuation node pads to a depth of $1500\text{ \AA}$. (Time: 8 Hours).
2.  **Dielectric Spin-Coating:** Spin-coat an amorphous fluoropolymer Cytop resin layer evenly over the gold control paths. Vacuum-bake at 80°C to secure a seamless 1.2-micron insulating shield with a 15V dielectric breakdown barrier. (Time: 6 Hours).
3.  **Solar Film Deposition:** Spray-apply the organic, non-toxic Perovskite-Chlorophyll photovoltaic matrix directly onto the vehicle’s aerodynamic outer biopolymer skin hull. Heat-seal to verify a continuous $220\text{ W/m}^2$ radiant solar harvesting yield. (Time: 10 Hours).

---

## 🛠️ Day 3: Component Integration, Pressure Carpets, & Transducer Bonding
1.  **Piezoelectric Ribbon Inlay:** Position the 250-micron quartz-crystal piezoelectric ribbons inside the dead-end deceleration tracts. Secure the flexible PVDF ribbon carpets flush across the 5-passenger seating bases and the 450-liter rear cargo hold boards. (Time: 8 Hours).
2.  **Initiation Grid Mounting:** Bond the micro-thin Platinum-Titanium heating grids into the base wheel-hub acceleration nodes using conductive silver epoxy paste. Mount the 12 standalone 2400 Hz acoustic levitation transducers directly flanking the wheel channels. (Time: 8 Hours).
3.  **Structural Potting and Shell Interlock:** Bolt and interlock the concentric wheel flywheel hub casings onto the monocoque bulkheads using zero-tool mechanical lock-tabs. Pot the wire interfaces inside a protective bio-rubber compound seal. (Time: 8 Hours).

---

## 🧪 Day 4: Vacuum Leak Decay Audit, Pure Water Charging, & System Sync
1.  **Hydrostatic Joint Decay Verification:** Seal the primary track loop manifold. Draw a deep vacuum of $-101.3\text{ kPa}$ and lock the line. Pressure decay loss must register completely flat ($\leq 0.05\text{ kPa}$) over a continuous 12-hour audit hold to certify a zero-consumption closed loop. (Time: 12 Hours).
2.  **Fluidic Bus Charging:** Vacuum-inject exactly 180.0 Liters of pure distilled water (45.0 Liters per wheel hub quadrant) into the track lines. Evacuate all air pockets to ensure solid hydraulic continuity. (Time: 4 Hours).
3.  **Resonant Static Ignition Check:** Deliver a 5000V test pulse from the graphene ultracapacitors to verify the 1600:1 flash-expansion push. Confirm that water re-condensation resolves in $\leq 2.0\text{ ms}$. Ramp up the simulated velocity to 100 km/h to confirm the 5:1 Venturi intakes drive the loop at a self-sustaining 3,789.4 RPM with zero water loss. System cleared for active service. (Time: 8 Hours).
