## 🏎️ 1. Reworking the Engine: From Gasoline to Pure Geometry

In a standard modern car, torque (the raw rotational force that turns your wheels) is generated by a series of controlled chemical explosions:
1.  **The Input:** A fine mist of toxic gasoline fuel is sprayed into a closed metal cylinder beneath a heavy piston.
2.  **The Ignition:** A spark plug ignites the gasoline, creating an explosive burst of chemical heat.
3.  **The Force:** The heat causes the air inside the cylinder to expand rapidly, violently pushing the heavy piston down to turn a metal crankshaft and axles.
4.  **The Waste:** Because this is a destructive chemical reaction, the fuel is permanently consumed and thrown out of the tailpipe as toxic exhaust.

**Project AETHERIS uses the exact same physical expansion loop, but replaces the destructive chemical explosion with an immortal, closed-loop water phase-change.**
