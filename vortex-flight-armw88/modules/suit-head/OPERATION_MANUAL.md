# Module suit-head: Ingress, Egress, Operation & Maintenance Manual

This manual governs the physical deployment, user-interface protocols, flight operation, and field maintenance cycles for the **Project ARMW-88 Wearable Head Flight Chassis**. These directives must be committed to memory by the pilot and crew before cleanroom deployment or flight envelope authorization.

## 🦾 1. Ingress (Donning) Protocol

To achieve a 100% secure, EMP-proof seal, execution must match these exact sequential steps:

1.  **Decontaminate Interface Rim:** Wipe down the interlocking tongue-and-groove Faraday tracks with a microfiber cloth to clear airborne grit or dust.
2.  **Separate Clamshell Halves:** Press both lower base buttons to pull the internal locking rods clear. Gently split the front and rear helmet frames apart.
3.  **Position Rear Substrate:** Place the rear helmet section flush against the back of the skull, aligning the lower neck flange with the upper spine plate connector of the torso harness.
4.  **Hinge Front Visor Face:** Swing the front face section downward over the brow, guiding the inner groove tracks to fully encapsulate the rear section's guide tongue.
5.  **Lock Jawline Detents:** Press the front and rear halves together at the chin until the twin 8mm stainless steel detent pins snap shut with an audible metallic click.
6.  **Verify Optical Alignment:** Look through the **Project ARMO-88** metalens window. Confirm your pupil center lines align perfectly with the target optical origin crosshairs.

## 🚨 2. Egress (Doffing) & Emergency Extraction Protocol

Standard extraction or high-stress emergency egress must be achievable entirely by the pilot using blind muscle memory:

*   **Standard Egress Sequence:** Reach both hands to the lower baseline collar rim. Press and hold the twin quick-release buttons simultaneously. Pull the front face forward and upward to clear the nose bridge, then slide the rear section smoothly off the skull.
*   **Emergency Extraction Time Constraint:** The pilot must practice this cycle until total extraction from a fully locked state is achieved in **under 2.0 seconds** in absolute darkness.
*   **Secondary Crew Override:** In an extraction event where the pilot is incapacitated, crew members can insert a 4mm pin tool directly into the recessed side override ports to mechanically force the detent springs open from the outside.

## 🧼 5. Biomimetic Liner Maintenance, Extraction & Wash Protocol

Because the integrated $14.0\text{ mm}$ second-skin liner rests in direct contact with the pilot’s primary head garment, it accumulates continuous moisture and skin oils that can obstruct the passive micro-thermal vents:

1.  **Extract the Substrate:** Ensure the helmet clamshell is open and unpinned. Reach into the internal cavity, pinch the lower neck collar rim of the flexible TPU liner, and pull inward toward the center axis to disengage the four lock pins from the shell.
2.  **Inspect Thermal Vent Lines:** Hold the elastic liner up to a direct cleanroom light array. Verify that all horizontal $2.4\text{ mm}$ cooling channels are completely free of organic grit, lint, or particulate blockages.
3.  **Chemical Wash Ingestion:** Submerge the flexible TPU liner in a 10% solution of isopropyl alcohol or medical-grade skin-safe enzymatic cleaner. Agitate gently by hand for 5 minutes. Do not use high-temperature autoclaves or harsh petroleum solvents, as they will cause the 30A elastomeric structures to prematurely warp or degrade.
4.  **Dry and Reseat Matrix:** Allow the liner to air-dry completely inside a dust-free staging chamber for a minimum of 2 hours. Align the push-pin tabs with the receptor ports of the carbon-fiber Faraday shell and snap firmly back into place.
