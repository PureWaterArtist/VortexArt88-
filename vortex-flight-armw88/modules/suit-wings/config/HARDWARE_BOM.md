# Module suit-wings: Physical Hardware Bill of Materials (HARDWARE_BOM.md)

This document governs the physical procurement specifications for the **Project ARMW-88 Morphing Aero-Resonator Wing Structure** and auxiliary components. All sourced elements and raw stocks must meet these rigid definitions to guarantee flight spar structural integrity, full-spectrum Faraday cage grounding, and passive emergency mechanical release survivability.

## 🧱 1. Wing Spars & Faraday Shielding Stocks

*   **Primary Material Stock:** 12.0kg Industrial Spool of High-Modulus Aerospace-Grade Carbon Fiber Polycarbonate (PC-CF) filament.
*   **Interleave Doping Medium:** Conductive Hexagonal Graphene Nanopowder Masterbatch matrix (minimum 99% pure carbon crystal grid layout).
*   **System Function:** Forms the rigid central spine root mounting box, structural rib linkages, and long primary wing spars, acting as a high-strength structural lift framework and continuous Faraday cage to dump high-voltage lightning surge fields.

*   **Energy Harvesting Surface Mesh:** Micro-grooved Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) fluoropolymer mesh sheeting ($1.0\text{ mm}$ thickness profile).

*   **Outer Camouflage Medium:** 90mL Solution of Synthesized High-Purity Guanine Photonic Nanocrystals ($130\text{ nm}$ lattice baseline pitch) suspended inside a polyurethanic elastic hydrogel base vehicle.
*   **System Function:** Fills the external wing micro-etched channels to build a $450\text{V}$ static field potential across the $EGaIn$ lines. The outer photonic hydrogel layer manually compresses or relaxes under wrist-twist inputs to execute full visible camouflage matching.

## 🔩 4. Mechanical Fasteners & Emergency Jettison Hardware

*   **Emergency Jettison Tension Cable:** 1x Inox 316L Marine Grade 4mm Bowden Tension Line featuring a stainless steel inner wire strand core.

*   **System Function:** Hooks directly into the front chest plate safety lever to form the mechanical emergency release matrix, separating the wing root structure completely from the pilot in under 0.5 seconds during a critical flight emergency.
