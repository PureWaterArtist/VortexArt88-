# Module suit-arms: Ingress, Egress, Operation & Maintenance Manual

This manual governs the physical deployment, user-interface protocols, flight operation, and field maintenance cycles for the **Project ARMW-88 Wearable Arm Flight Chassis**. These directives must be committed to memory by the pilot and crew before cleanroom deployment or flight envelope authorization.

---

## 🦾 1. Ingress (Donning) Protocol

To achieve a 100% secure link to the torso and wing tracks, execution must match these exact sequential steps:

1.  **Inspect Slide Tracks:** Run a visual scan down the external carbon-fiber linear sliding rails. Ensure the tracks are entirely clear of dirt, dust, or residual hydrogel.
2.  **Slide Into Arm Sleeves:** Guide the hand and forearm through the bicep cuff, pushing through the flexible TPU elbow bellows until the wrist settles firmly against the control handle registry.
3.  **Engage Shoulder Sockets:** Raise the arm assembly upward, sliding the upper connection tongue into the torso's shoulder receptor wells.
4.  **Lock Socket Detents:** Push the $16.0\text{ mm}$ hardened steel quick-release detent pins completely through the horizontal frame alignment bores. Listen for an audible metallic click.
5.  **Verify Joint Clearance:** Flex the elbow joint through its full range of motion. Confirm that the flexible TPU accordion bellows expand and compress smoothly without pinching skin or flight garments.
6.  **Calibrate Wrist Registry:** Cycle your wrist $45^\circ$ clockwise and counterclockwise. Confirm that the mechanical pneumatic control valves click cleanly to verify connection to the outer camouflage system.

## 🚨 2. Egress (Doffing) & Emergency Extraction Protocol

Standard extraction or high-stress emergency jettison must be achievable entirely by the pilot using blind muscle memory:

*   **Standard Egress Sequence:** Reach across the chest with the opposite hand. Press and hold the push button on the $16.0\text{ mm}$ shoulder detent pin. Pull the locking rod completely free. Slide your limb backward out of the forearm sleeve, clearing the framework.
*   **Emergency Extraction Time Constraint:** The pilot must practice this sequence until total arm module separation and extraction is achieved in **under 2.5 seconds** per limb in absolute darkness.
*   **Secondary Crew Override:** In an extraction event where the pilot is incapacitated, ground crew members can pull the bright red exterior pull-rings attached to the base of the shoulder pins to mechanically eject the arm sleeve units instantly.

## 🌀 3. Flight Operation & Interface Mechanics

Because this arm framework is 100% solid-state and non-electronic, operational parameters are controlled entirely via physical mechanical inputs:

1.  **Active Wing Morphing Actuation:** The suit does not use electric motors or servo systems. To extend the wings into a high-aspect albatross soaring configuration, extend your forearms forward and outward. The sliding joints of the wing skeleton will ride along your forearm's **Linear Sliding Guide Tracks**, locking the airfoil into position. Drawing your elbows inward pulls the tracks back, altering the wing sweep angle for high-speed diving maneuvers.
2.  **Triboelectric Static Siphoning:** Energy recycling occurs automatically during high-speed flight. As wind shear forces air friction across the outer skin at 200 km/h, the sliding micro-ridges on the **Triboelectric Fluoropolymer Mesh** continuously generate a high-voltage static potential field. This charge does not feed a digital battery; it is directly siphoned down micro-fluidic channels filled with a conductive liquid gallium alloy to actuate downstream mechanical valves and joint dampeners.

## 🔧 4. Field Preventive Maintenance (PM) Ledger

To ensure the arm assemblies maintain their structural integrity and track alignment over their operational life cycle, execute these baseline checks every **50 flight hours**:

| Check Target | Operational Inspection Metric | Field Corrective Action Protocol |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Linear Guide Rails** | Inspect the carbon-fiber sliding tracks for groove widening, structural burrs, or scoring. | Smooth out minor track blemishes with fine abrasive paper; coat the tracks with dry graphite powder. |
| **Triboelectric Mesh** | Check the outer fluoropolymer mesh for surface tearing, contamination, or localized abrasion. | Clean the mesh face with filtered isopropyl alcohol; clear out dirt blocking the micro-ridges. |
| **Elbow Accordion Bellows** | Scan the flexible TPU elbow sections for splitting, puncture signs, or material fatigue creases. | Unsnap the compromised joint casing; replace with a fresh, continuous 3D-printed flexible TPU sleeve. |
| **Shoulder Socket Pins** | Test the compression resistance and return spring speed of the 16mm hardened steel locking pins. | Flush the internal pin cylinder with dry graphite micro-powder. Never use petroleum-based greases. |
