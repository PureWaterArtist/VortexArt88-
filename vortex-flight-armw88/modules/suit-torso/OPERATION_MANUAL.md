## 🦾 1. Ingress (Donning) Protocol

1.  **Doff Head and Arm Assemblies:** Ensure the helmet and modular arm sleeves are detached and clear of the main torso framework.
2.  **Release Central Chest Dial:** Rotate the front central chest locking dial $90^\circ$ counterclockwise. This mechanically draws the dual-redundant steel alignment bars inward, unlocking the side frame latch receptors.
3.  **Open Clamshell Framework:** Swing the front chest panel outward along the heavy-duty integrated side hinge pins, opening the chassis to its maximum clearance angle.
4.  **Step Into Rear Backplate:** Back the pilot's torso flush into the rear backplate assembly, aligning the shoulder curves with the upper shoulder sockets and settling the base rim onto the hip support zone.
5.  **Secure Front Clamshell Face:** Close the front chest plate tightly over the rib cage. Ensure the internal $18.0\text{ mm}$ flexible elastomer liner compresses uniformly against the pilot's protective flight garment without pinching or folding.
6.  **Engage Central Lock Dial:** Force the front and back plates completely flush, then rotate the central mechanical chest dial $90^\circ$ clockwise. Listen for a loud, metallic double-click as the steel locking bars throw outward into the side frame receptors.
7.  **Run Physical Seam Audit:** Visually check the lateral interlocking seams. Verify that the front and back plates show a completely zero-gap structural mating configuration across all axes.

## 🚨 2. Egress (Doffing) & Emergency Extraction Protocol

Standard extraction or high-stress emergency egress must be achievable entirely by the pilot using blind muscle memory, completely independent of external support:

*   **Standard Egress Sequence:** Place the palm flat against the central chest dial. Press inward firmly to disengage the primary safety retention detent, then twist the dial $90^\circ$ counterclockwise. Push the front chest panel forward along its hinges and step clear of the backplate framework.
*   **Emergency Extraction Time Constraint:** The pilot must practice this cycle until total extraction from a fully locked torso state is achieved in **under 3.5 seconds** in absolute darkness.
*   **Secondary Crew Override:** In an extraction event where the pilot is incapacitated, crew members can insert a heavy-duty T-handle key directly into the recessed rear mechanical override ports on the backplate. Turning the key $90^\circ$ manually overrides the chest link-bars, splitting the clamshell open instantly.
