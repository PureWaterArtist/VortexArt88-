# Module suit-legs: Ingress, Egress, Operation & Maintenance Manual

This manual governs the physical deployment, user-interface protocols, flight operation, and field maintenance cycles for the **Project ARMW-88 Wearable Leg & Landing Gear Chassis**. These directives must be committed to memory by the pilot and crew before cleanroom deployment or flight envelope authorization.

---

## 🦾 1. Ingress (Donning) Protocol

To achieve a 100% secure link to the torso and full structural load-bearing readiness, execution must match these exact sequential steps:

1.  **Decontaminate Retention Mounts:** Wipe down the dual hip detent attachment tracks with a microfiber cloth to clear field grit or residual hydrogel.
2.  **Slide Into Leg Chassis:** Guide the leg through the upper thigh guard and lower shin sleeve collar, settling the pilot's flight boot firmly into the heavy-duty structural ankle interface plate.
3.  **Engage Hip Interlocks:** Raise the leg assembly upward to interface the upper load-bearing tongue with the torso’s bottom base rim receptors.
4.  **Lock Hip Detents:** Slide the Inox 316L stainless steel quick-release locking pins completely through the transverse alignment channels. Listen for a loud, metallic click.
5.  **Verify Leaf-Loop Clearance:** Execute a deep knee bend and flex the ankle structure. Confirm that the multi-layered **Kangaroo-Tendon Leaf-Spring Loops** at the lower shin and heel maintain a clear $150\text{ mm}$ travel corridor without hitting external armor plates.
6.  **Calibrate Ankle Strain Bus:** Stomp the heel firmly against the workbench matting. Verify that the fluidic pressure indicators on the lower calf plate register full baseline pressurization, confirming linkage to the core power bus lines.

## 🚨 2. Egress (Doffing) & Emergency Extraction Protocol

Standard extraction or high-stress emergency jettison must be achievable entirely by the pilot using blind muscle memory, completely independent of external support:

*   **Standard Egress Sequence:** Reach down to the left and right hip baseline collar parameters. Press and hold the push buttons on the quick-release locking pins. Pull the detent rods completely free. Slide your limbs upward out of the thigh and shin collars, stepping clear of the lower framework.
*   **Emergency Extraction Time Constraint:** The pilot must practice this sequence until total lower-limb module separation and extraction is achieved in **under 3.0 seconds** in absolute darkness.
*   **Secondary Crew Override:** In an emergency extraction scenario where the pilot is incapacitated, crew members can hook extraction tethers directly into the external quick-release ring loops on the hip pins, pulling them clear to manually eject the leg frames instantly.

## 🌀 3. Flight Operation & Interface Mechanics

Because this leg framework is 100% solid-state and non-electronic, operational parameters are controlled entirely via passive physics and physical movements:

1.  **Passive Kinetic Landing Mitigation:** The suit does not use active hydraulic dampening pumps or digital terrain triggers. Ground impact protection runs continuously via material mechanics. During rough-field touchdowns at vertical sink rates up to $4.5\text{ m/s}$, the multi-layered carbon fiber leaf-spring loops flex smoothly, absorbing the high-percussive $125,000\text{ N/m}$ ground force vector. This kinetic mass is redirected and dissipated safely across the pilot's core torso frame, bypassing skeletal loading.
2.  **Mechanochemical Camouflage Operation:** The stealth skin runs on non-electronic mechanochemical physics driven by the pilot's natural thigh stride and knee flex cycles. In flight cruise, the guanine nanocrystal lattice rests at a baseline $130\text{ nm}$ pitch (reflecting environmental greens/blues). If a sharp camouflage alteration is required to match changing ground patterns, the pilot can shift their heel angle inward $15^\circ$ to mechanically engage the calf pneumatic pump valves, expanding the hydrogel thickness to hold an earthy camouflage reflection footprint with zero electrical power.
