# Module suit-legs: Cleanroom Post-Processing & Landing Gear Quality Manual

This operational standard governs the verification protocols, post-processing steps, and impact deceleration metrics for the **Project ARMW-88 Wearable Leg & Landing Gear Chassis**. These audits must be completed sequentially to certify structural lower-limb integrity and leaf-loop force containment before the landing gear module is cleared for flight operations.

---

## 🔬 1. Post-Print Shell Processing & Leaf-Loop Alignment
*   **Leaf-Spring Integrity Audit:** Scan the custom-molded multi-layered pre-preg carbon fiber leaf loops under backlit optical magnification. Check for layer gaps, resin bubbles, or fiber fraying. Any localized defect will cause immediate delamination under a \(125,000\text{ N/m}\) structural payload.
*   **Ankle Interface Dimension Check:** Measure the interior opening parameters of the heavy-duty ankle interface plate using digital calipers. Verify accuracy within \(\pm 0.05\text{ mm}\). The pilot's flight boot must lock completely flat into the latch receptors without mechanical wiggle.
*   **Linear Clearances Verification:** Test the travel arc corridor of the shin and heel leaf loops. Compress the spring loops mechanically down to their \(150\text{ mm}\) limit; verify that the carbon ribbons do not score or contact the rigid outer protective armor plates.

---

## 🧲 2. Faraday Grid Conductivity & Camouflage Calibration
*   **Faraday Resistance Matrix Check:** Map electrical path continuity by placing digital multimeter test probes across the extreme vertices of the thigh guard down to the ankle collar. The graphene-infused carbon-fiber polycarbonate matrix must demonstrate a continuous electrical path under \(12\text{ \Omega}\) across all surfaces to ensure complete EMP grounding capability.
*   **Chameleon Hydrogel Injection Matrix:** Inject exactly \(65\text{ mL}\) of the calibrated sub-micron guanine crystal elastomeric hydrogel matrix into the external \(1.5\text{ mm}\) macro-groove skin tracks. Use vacuum-purging protocols to completely eliminate airborne bubble pockets within the channels.
*   **Heel Pneumatic Pump Check:** Cycle the lower calf pneumatic pump valves using a manual test syringe. Verify that the outer photonic hydrogel layer expands uniformly across the thigh and shin panels, shifting the reflected color band smoothly across the visible spectrum.

---

## 🩺 3. High-Impact Drop-Tower & Structural Load Testing

*   **Kangaroo-Tendon Kinetic Drop Test:** Mount the completed lower leg exoskeleton framework into a high-impact drop-tower testing rig weighted to a \(100\text{ kg}\) dummy payload profile. Release the assembly from a vertical fall height calibrated to simulate a sudden \(4.5\text{ m/s}\) sink rate touchdown. The multi-layered carbon fiber leaf-spring loops must compress smoothly, completely absorbing the high-percussive \(860.0\text{ Joules}\) ground force vector without micro-cracking.
*   **Skeletal Torso Load Routing Check:** Connect a series of dynamic load-cell sensors along the upper hip connection interfaces during the drop-tower test. Confirm that the kinetic shock wave is cleanly routed and dissipated outward across the torso harness frames, ensuring zero percussive load spikes bleed inward to the inner pilot leg lining.
*   **Hip Interface Pin Pull Test:** Apply a severe multi-axial pulling force line up to \(3000\text{ Newtons}\) directly to the \(14.0\text{ mm}\) Inox 316L stainless steel quick-release detent pins. The locking assembly must sustain the load with absolute zero physical distortion or retention groove wear.
