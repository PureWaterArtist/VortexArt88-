# ARFC-88 Cleanroom Wafer Fabrication & Symmetrical Bonding Manual
### Standard Operating Protocols for Solid-State Fluidic Logic Micro-Circuits

This manual documents the mandatory step-by-step substrate cleaning, Deep-Reactive Ion Etching (DRIE), and laser diffusion wafer bonding protocols required to assemble the **Project ARFC-88 Fluidic Computer**. All procedures must be executed within a certified **ISO Class 5 (Class 100) Cleanroom Environment** to prevent microscopic organic dust or particulate contaminants from entering the sub-millimeter channels, which would cause immediate boundary layer separation, block the $0.15\text{mm}$ control nozzles, and disrupt Boolean switching logic under peak velocity loads.

---

## 🧼 1. Multi-Stage Substrate Preparation & DRIE Etching

Prior to etching the micro-fluidic logic gates, the raw quartz crystal slices must undergo an absolute chemical and geometric purification pass:

1.  **Piranha Etch Decontamination:** Submerge the **Optical-Grade Monocrystalline Quartz Wafers** inside a fresh bath of Piranha solution (3:1 concentrated Sulfuric Acid $\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4$ to Hydrogen Peroxide $\text{H}_2\text{O}_2$) for 20 minutes at $120^\circ\text{C}$ to completely strip all organic molecular residue. Rinse continuously with Type I Ultra-Pure Deionized Water.
2.  **Photolithography Masking:** Spin-coat a uniform 2.5-micron layer of positive photoresist onto the Z-cut quartz face. Expose the substrate beneath an industrial mask aligner utilizing the exact spatial vectors generated by `switch_engine.py` and `and_engine.py`. Develop the resist to expose the target channel boundaries.
3.  **Deep-Reactive Ion Etching (DRIE):** Mount the masked quartz wafer inside an inductively coupled plasma DRIE chamber. Run a continuous fluorine-based ($\text{SF}_6/\text{C}_4\text{F}_8$) Bosch process plasma sweep until all logic channels, Coanda walls, and Figure-8 conjunction intersections reach a uniform vertical trench depth of exactly **$250\text{ microns}$** ($\pm 1\mu\text{m}$).
4.  **Oxygen Plasma Strip:** Strip the remaining photoresist mask inside an oxygen plasma ash chamber, followed by a final mega-sonic deionized water rinse to hit an absolute internal channel surface roughness finish target of **$\text{Ra } 0.02\text{ microns}$**.

---

## 🔩 2. Solid-State Layer Integration & Energy Jacket Placement

Once etching is completed, assemble the multi-tier solid-state computational stack using non-magnetic, oil-free titanium tweezers:

1.  **Manifold Screen-Printing:** Mount the CNC micro-machined **PEEK Optima Polymer Backing Plate** onto a cleanroom printing fixture. Screen-print a highly uniform, 15-micron pattern of high-temperature **Polyimide Structural Bonding Resin** over the mating flange interfaces, keeping all inlet ports clear.
2.  **PVDF Resonance Stack Layering:** Position the **Piezoelectric PVDF Metallized Film Sheets** into the designated recess channel behind the quartz logic wafer block. Secure the contact tabs to the positive and negative trace terminals using a thin swipe of conductive silver-epoxy.
3.  **Seebeck Thermal Pellet Grid:** Drop the 16 pairs of **Bismuth Telluride ($\text{Bi}_2\text{Te}_3$) Thermoelectric Micro-Pellets** into their concentric tracking pockets along the outer perimeter frame wall. Ensure the gold-plated copper contacts interface cleanly with your power collection rail.

---

## ⚡ 3. Laser Diffusion Wafer Bonding & Hermetic Sealing

To handle continuous $45\text{ PSI}$ fluid logic streams and eliminate any inter-gate gas leakage without adding mechanical screws that would warp the optical wafer channels, you must execute a localized thermal diffusion weld:

1.  **Wafer Stack Clamping:** Bring the etched monocrystalline quartz logic wafer and the top optical quartz window window face-to-face. Place the assembly inside a pneumatic cleanroom bonding press. Apply a perfectly uniform, continuous **$2.5\text{ kN}$ physical clamping force** across the surface grid area.
2.  **Laser Diffusion Welding:** Align a continuous-wave **Carbon Dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$) Infrared Laser** ($10.6\mu\text{m}$ wavelength, $30\mu\text{m}$ spot point focus) over the stack. Trace the laser beam precisely along the non-channel border perimeters. The localized infrared radiation passes straight through the top layer to melt the contact boundaries of the underlying quartz tracks, fusing the layers into a seamless, solid-state block.
3.  **Thermal Post-Cure:** Transfer the bonded quartz block into a cleanroom oven. Ramp the temperature to $180^\circ\text{C}$ and hold for 2 hours to fully cure the polyimide backing plates, achieving a gas-tight, completely leak-free seal rated to withstand over **$2,500\text{ PSI}$ of internal burst resistance**.

## 🚦 4. Post-Assembly System Readiness Checkout

Before piping dry Nitrogen gas or powering up the opto-electronic telemetry matrix, execute this multi-point automated checkout routine:

*   **The Opto-Alignment Optical Audit:** Place the bonded computing block beneath a digital metrology camera. Verify that the **940nm Infrared Emitters and Phototransistors** inside the aluminum ARFC-03 casing align perfectly centered over the 250-micron etched exit tracks within a spatial dimensional tolerance window of **$<0.01\text{mm}$**.
*   **The Pneumatic Flow Continuity Check:** Pipe high-purity dry Nitrogen gas into the primary input ports. Slow-ramp inlet static head pressure up to **$65\text{ PSI}$**. Use an electronic micro-flow sensor to verify that the internal power jets successfully hit a minimum baseline velocity of **$32.5\text{ m/s}$** across all checkpoints with **absolute zero inter-channel cross-leakage or back-pressure drop**, confirming an error-free, EMP-immune logic twin.
